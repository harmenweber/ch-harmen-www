Quotations, Citations, and Blockquotes

There are three major HTML elements involved in quotations: <q/> <cite/>, and <blockquote/>.

Quotations

<q/> elements are for inline quotes. Here is a quote: I could hand out cupcakes to keep them interested.

Citations

The <cite/> element should be used for referencing creative work rather than the person who said or wrote the quote. In other words, itâ€™s not for quotes. Blockquotes

Blockquote tags are used for distinguishing quoted text from the rest of the content. Quotes of four lines or longer should be set apart this way. Here is an example:

How can I keep the audience engaged with this article on quotations? Grammar and a charming personality will only go so far.

A blockquote can hold further HTML elements. To please the eye, we could wrap the blockquote into a panel div that contains the blockquote and the person who said or wrote the quote. Here is an example:
